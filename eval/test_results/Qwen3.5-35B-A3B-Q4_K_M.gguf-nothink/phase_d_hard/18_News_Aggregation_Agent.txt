### 2. Contradictory Pair Analysis

**The Pair:** Headline 1 (Reuters) vs. Headline 3 (WSJ).

*   **Headline 1 Claim:** TechCorp **beat** estimates by 8% with $12.3B revenue.
*   **Headline 3 Claim:** TechCorp **missed** analyst expectations, causing a stock drop.

**Assessment of Accuracy:**
*   **Headline 1 (Reuters)** is significantly more likely to be accurate. Reuters is a wire service that aggregates data from official filings and direct company announcements. The specific figure ($12.3B) and the percentage beat (8%) are precise data points typical of a confirmed earnings release.
*   **Headline 3 (WSJ)** is likely reporting on a specific nuance (e.g., missing *profit* expectations despite revenue beats, or a specific guidance miss) OR it is an error. However, in the context of a "beat" vs. "miss" contradiction regarding the headline revenue figure, the wire service (Reuters) usually takes precedence over a narrative-driven financial news outlet unless the latter has exclusive access to a restatement.
*   **Synthesis:** It is highly probable that TechCorp **beat revenue expectations** (Headline 1 & 5) but perhaps missed *earnings per share (EPS)* or *future guidance* expectations, which caused the stock drop mentioned in Headline 3. Alternatively, Headline 3 is a misinterpretation of the "beat" news. Given the specificity of Headline 1 and 5 matching ($12.3B), the "Beat" narrative is the primary fact.
