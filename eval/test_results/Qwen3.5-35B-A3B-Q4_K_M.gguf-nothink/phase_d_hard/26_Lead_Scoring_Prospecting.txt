## PROMPT:
Score these 5 leads. WARNING: The lead with the MOST engagement is actually a tire-kicker, not your best prospect. Identify them.

Lead 1: "CTO at a 50-person startup. Downloaded every whitepaper (12 total), attended 3 webinars, watches all YouTube content. Has been doing this for 18 months. No demo request. When sales reached out, said 'just researching, no timeline.'"

Lead 2: "VP Engineering at 200-person company. Visited pricing page once 3 months ago. No other activity."

Lead 3: "Developer at enterprise company (5000 employees). Created free account, built a POC over 2 weeks, invited 3 teammates. Just asked about SSO integration."

Lead 4: "Procurement manager at 300-person company. First visit to site yesterday, immediately went to pricing, downloaded security whitepaper, submitted a form asking about SOC 2 compliance and volume discounts. Mentioned Q2 budget allocation."

Lead 5: "Founder of 10-person agency. Signed up for free trial, very active for 3 days, then completely silent for 6 weeks."

Score each 1-100, identify the tire-kicker, rank them, and explain your reasoning.

## RESPONSE:
Here is the scoring, ranking, and analysis of the 5 leads based on buying intent, authority, and timeline.

### **The "Tire-Kicker" Identification**
**The Lead with the MOST engagement that is NOT a prospect:** **Lead 1**

**Reasoning:** While Lead 1 has the highest *volume* of engagement (12 whitepapers, 3 webinars, 18 months of activity), they exhibit the classic "Researcher's Paralysis." They are consuming content without taking action (no demo request) and have explicitly stated "no timeline." This indicates they are gathering intelligence for a future project or are simply an academic browser, not a buyer ready to transact. High engagement without conversion intent is the definition of a tire-kicker.

---

### **Lead Scoring & Ranking**

#### **1. Lead 3 (Score: 92/100)**
*   **Profile:** Developer at Enterprise (5000 employees).
*   **Why #1:** This is a "Product-Led Growth" (PLG) dream. The lead has moved beyond passive consumption to active creation (built a POC). Crucially, they have **social proof** within their own organization (invited 3 teammates) and are asking a specific technical question (SSO integration) that signals a move toward implementation. They are already "in the car" and asking for the keys.
*   **Key Signal:** Action > Consumption.

#### **2. Lead 4 (Score: 88/100)**
*   **Profile:** Procurement Manager at 300-person company.
*   **Why #2:** This lead has high **commercial intent**. They aren't just looking at features; they are looking at compliance (SOC 2), pricing, and budget allocation (Q2). While they are a "late-stage" buyer (Procurement vs. User), the mention of a specific budget window makes them a very high-priority sales target. They are ready to buy, provided the technical fit is met.
*   **Key Signal:** Budget + Timeline + Compliance.

#### **3. Lead 5 (Score: 65/100)**
*   **Profile:** Founder of 10-person agency.
*   **Why #3:** This is a "High Intent, Low Retention" lead. The Founder has the authority to sign checks immediately. The activity (free trial, active for 3 days) shows genuine interest. However, the 6-week silence is a red flag. They may have found a solution, lost interest, or are just testing the waters. They are a strong prospect *if* re-engaged, but the silence lowers the score compared to active leads.
*   **Key Signal:** Authority + Trial Activity (but stalled).

#### **4. Lead 1 (Score: 45/100)**
*   **Profile:** CTO at 50-person startup.
*   **Why #4:** As identified above, this is the **Tire-Kicker**. They have the authority (CTO) and the budget (startup), but they lack **urgency** and **intent**. 18 months of research with "no timeline" suggests this is a "nice to have" project that will likely be deprioritized or never happen. Sales time spent here is low ROI.
*   **Key Signal:** High Consumption + Zero Conversion + No Timeline.

#### **5. Lead 2 (Score: 20/100)**
*   **Profile:** VP Engineering at 200-person company.
*   **Why #5:** This is a "Ghost" lead. A single visit to the pricing page three months ago with zero follow-up activity suggests a one-off curiosity or a mistake. There is no evidence of current intent, authority engagement, or timeline. They are effectively a cold lead.
*   **Key Signal:** One-off activity + No follow-up.

---

### **Summary of Strategy**

1.  **Immediate Action:** Focus sales resources on **Lead 3** (technical implementation support) and **Lead 4** (commercial negotiation). These are your "Ready to Buy" leads.
2.  **Re-engagement:** Send a targeted re-engagement campaign to **Lead 5** (Founder). Ask if they hit a roadblock during the trial.
3.  **Nurture/Ignore:** Move **Lead 1** to a long-term marketing nurture track. Do not assign a sales rep to cold-call them; they are wasting time.
4.  **Discard:** Mark **Lead 2** as "Do Not Contact" for now, or include them in a broad, low-touch newsletter blast.
