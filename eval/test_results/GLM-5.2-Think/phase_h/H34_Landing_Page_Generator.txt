      <div>
        <div class="section-num">01 — Features</div>
        <h2 class="section-title">An editor that reads<br/>before it <em>rewrites</em>.</h2>
      </div>
      <p class="section-lede">Four tools that work the way a thoughtful copy editor does — quietly, in the margin, only speaking when it has something worth saying.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="features">
      <article class="feat f1 reveal">
        <span class="tag">Core</span>
        <div class="icon" aria-hidden="true">
          <svg width="22" height="22" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.4"><path d="M4 19h16M6 15l3-9 3 9 3-7 3 7"/></svg>
        </div>
        <h3>Cadence & rhythm</h3>
        <p>QuillAI reads your sentences aloud, silently. It flags where the rhythm stumbles — too many spondees, a clause that runs out of breath — and suggests the smallest possible fix.</p>
      </article>

      <article class="feat f2 reveal">
        <span class="tag">Voice</span>
        <div class="icon" aria-hidden="true">
          <svg width="22" height="22" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.4"><circle cx="12" cy="12" r="3.2"/><path d="M5 12a7 7 0 0 1 14 0M8 12a4 4 0 0 1 8 0"/></svg>
        </div>
        <h3>Voice preservation</h3>
        <p>Trained on your last 20 documents, QuillAI learns the way you comma, the words you reach for, the ones you'd never use. Edits stay inside your register.</p>
      </article>

      <article class="feat f3 reveal">
        <span class="tag">Draft</span>
        <div class="icon" aria-hidden="true">
          <svg width="22" height="22" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.4"><path d="M5 21l4-1 11-11-3-3L6 17l-1 4z"/><path d="M15 6l3 3"/></svg>
        </div>
        <h3>Inline suggestions</h3>
        <p>Margin notes, not rewrites. Every suggestion shows the why — the rule, the reference, the alternative — so you keep the pen.</p>
      </article>

      <article class="feat f4 reveal">
        <span class="tag">Research</span>
        <div class="icon" aria-hidden="true">
          <svg width="22" height="22" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.4"><path d="M12 3v18M5 8l7-5 7 5M5 16l7 5 7-5"/></svg>
        </div>
        <h3>Citation & fact-check</h3>
        <p>Every claim that should have a source gets a small mark in the margin. QuillAI checks 14 reference corpora and flags anything it can't verify — without inventing citations, ever.</p>
      </article>

<!-- ============ HOW IT WORKS ============ -->
<section class="block how" id="how" aria-labelledby="how-title">
  <div class="wrap">
    <div class="section-head reveal">
      <div>
        <div class="section-num">02 — How it works</div>
        <h2 class="section-title" id="how-title">Three steps, then<br/>you forget it's <em>there</em>.</h2>
      </div>
      <p class="section-lede">QuillAI is designed to disappear into your existing editor. No new tab, no context switch, no learning curve worth mentioning.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="steps">
      <div class="step reveal">
        <div class="step-line" aria-hidden="true"></div>
        <div class="step-num">i.</div>
        <h3>Connect your draft</h3>
        <p>Paste in, or install the extension for Google Docs, Notion, Obsidian, and a plain-markdown desktop app. QuillAI reads the whole document for context before suggesting a single comma.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="step reveal">
        <div class="step-line" aria-hidden="true"></div>
        <div class="step-num">ii.</div>
        <h3>Review the margin</h3>
        <p>Suggestions appear as quiet marginalia — accept, refine, or dismiss with a keystroke. Each one cites the principle behind it, so you learn the craft as you go, not just the fix.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="step reveal">
        <div class="step-line" aria-hidden="true"></div>
        <div class="step-num">iii.</div>
        <h3>Ship the cleaner draft</h3>
        <p>Export back to your editor with revisions accepted, or keep a clean diff for your collaborator. QuillAI never publishes, never trains on private work, never holds your text longer than the session.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

    <div class="faq reveal">
      <div class="faq-item">
        <button class="faq-q" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-a1">
          <span>Does QuillAI train on my writing?</span>
          <span class="plus" aria-hidden="true"></span>
        </button>
        <div class="faq-a" id="faq-a1" role="region"><div class="faq-a-inner">No. Your documents are processed in-session to produce suggestions and discarded when you close the tab. We never use customer text to train our models. Enterprise customers can additionally opt for a fully isolated, single-tenant deployment.</div></div>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <button class="faq-q" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-a2">
          <span>Which editors does it work inside?</span>
          <span class="plus" aria-hidden="true"></span>
        </button>
        <div class="faq-a" id="faq-a2" role="region"><div class="faq-a-inner">Google Docs, Notion, Obsidian, and a standalone desktop app for macOS, Windows, and Linux. A plain-markdown web editor is also available at app.quillai.ai. We're working on Word and Pages for Q2.</div></div>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <button class="faq-q" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-a3">
          <span>How is this different from Grammarly or ChatGPT?</span>
          <span class="plus" aria-hidden="true"></span>
        </button>
        <div class="faq-a" id="faq-a3" role="region"><div class="faq-a-inner">Grammarly is a grammar checker; it corrects errors. ChatGPT is a generator; it rewrites wholesale. QuillAI sits between — an editor that respects your voice and explains its reasoning. It suggests, cites, and steps back. It does not generate paragraphs on your behalf.</div></div>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <button class="faq-q" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-a4">
          <span>Can I use it for academic writing?</span>
          <span class="plus" aria-hidden="true"></span>
        </button>
        <div class="faq-a" id="faq-a4" role="region"><div class="faq-a-inner">Yes. QuillAI never invents citations — if it can't verify a claim against one of its 14 reference corpora, it flags the claim rather than fabricating a source. Many of our users are PhD students and journal editors. We're compliant with the COPE guidelines on authorship and AI assistance.</div></div>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <button class="faq-q" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-a5">
          <span>What happens to my data when I cancel?</span>

A few notes on the design choices, since they're load-bearing:

- **Palette**: warm parchment (`#f3ebdc`) + deep ink (`#1a1410`) + a single vermilion accent (`#c8431f`). No blue, no purple, no gradient — the page is supposed to feel like a well-printed magazine, not a SaaS dashboard. A subtle SVG grain overlay (multiply blend) gives the whole thing a paper texture.
- **Typography**: Fraunces (variable serif, optical-size 144 for display) carries every headline and the manuscript body; Inter handles UI. The italic accent in headlines is the only "decoration" — no `bg-clip-text` gradients, no glow.
- **Hero**: asymmetric 1.15/0.85 split. The right column is a "manuscript card" rotated -1.2° with a stacked shadow, showing a live typing effect on a real paragraph — so the hero demonstrates the product instead of describing it.
- **Before/after**: a real strikethrough/insertion diff on an actual bad paragraph, with a vertical "QuillAI" divider between the columns. This is constraint #20 done as content, not as a slider gimmick.
- **Pricing**: 3 tiers, the middle one inverted (ink background) with a small "Most chosen" flag — restrained, not a glowing badge.
- **Motion**: typing effect, IntersectionObserver scroll reveals, FAQ accordion, hover lifts on cards. All gated behind `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Accessibility**: semantic landmarks (`header/nav/main/section/footer`), `aria-label`/`aria-expanded`/`aria-controls` on the FAQ, `aria-live` on the typed region, alt-equivalent `aria-hidden` on decorative SVGs, labeled form fields, focus-visible inherited from defaults.

All 20 constraints are met — you can verify by searching the source for each anchor (`#features`, `#pricing`, `#testimonials`, `#contact`, the `meta name="description"`, the `<title>`, the newsletter form, the two distinct CTA labels "Start Free Trial" / "Get Started" / "See how it works", the "Trusted by 10,000+ writers" line, the 3-step How It Works, the before/after diff, and the email address `hello@quillai.ai`).
