**Overall Concept**  
A single‑product, “hero” shot that makes the mouse look like a high‑tech trophy. The mouse is the only object in the frame, positioned slightly off‑center to the right, with a gentle three‑quarter turn that shows the left side (the side with the RGB strip) and the top surface. The camera is low enough that the front of the mouse is just barely visible, emphasizing the sleek silhouette while still exposing the USB‑C port and the detachable paracord cable. The image is crisp, ultra‑sharp, and rendered with a shallow depth of field so the mouse is razor‑sharp and the background falls into a smooth, velvety blur.

---

### 1. Mouse Layout & Geometry  

| Feature | Visual Treatment |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Overall shape** | Ergonomic, slightly scooped shell. Dimensions are evident: 126 mm long, 64 mm wide, 40 mm tall. The mouse sits on a thin, invisible “airplane” stand that lifts it 2 mm off the surface, giving a floating effect. |
| **Matte‑black body** | The main chassis is a deep, non‑reflective matte black with a brushed‑metal micro‑texture (like fine sandpaper) that catches light only at grazing angles. No glossy spots; the surface looks like aerospace‑grade polymer. |
| **Honeycomb perforation** | The top surface is a precise honeycomb lattice, each cell about 2 mm across, with clean, sharp edges. The holes are slightly deeper than the surrounding material, creating a subtle shadow inside each cell that adds depth. Light from the key light spills into the cells, producing a faint “grid glow” that emphasizes the pattern. |
| **RGB side strip** | A thin, 2‑mm‑wide LED strip runs the entire length of the left side (the side facing the camera). It emits a pure cyan (RGB = 0,255,255) that is evenly saturated, with a soft diffusion that makes the strip look like a glowing vein. The cyan is bright enough to be the only color in the scene, but not so intense that it washes out the matte black. |
| **USB‑C charging port** | Center‑front, recessed 1 mm into the shell. The port’s metal housing is a brushed‑aluminum finish (light‑gray) that reflects a faint specular highlight. A tiny “USB‑C” icon is laser‑etched in matte black, invisible unless the light hits it directly. |
| **5 programmable buttons** | The left side hosts three side‑buttons (top, middle, bottom) and the right side has two thumb‑buttons. Each button is slightly concave, with a tactile “click” ridge. The button caps are the same matte black as the body, but the edges are finished in a semi‑gloss micro‑polymer that catches a thin rim light, giving a subtle “button‑glow” outline. |
| **DPI switch (top)** | A small, circular button on the very top‑center, 6 mm in diameter. The button is matte black with a tiny, recessed LED ring that glows cyan when the DPI mode is active. In the photo the LED is on, showing a thin cyan halo around the button. |
| **Weight indicator** | A tiny “67 g” engraving is laser‑etched on the underside of the mouse (visible only in the reflected rim light). It appears as a faint, glossy line that catches the rim light, hinting at the ultralight construction. |
| **Detachable cable** | A short length (≈15 cm) of braided paracord is shown coiled loosely around the left side of the mouse, with the connector plugged into the USB‑C port. The paracord is dark‑gray with a subtle, woven texture; each strand is visible, and the ends have a matte‑black snap‑fit connector. The cable’s ends are slightly illuminated by the key light, showing the texture without dominating the composition. |
| **Scale reference** | A US quarter (24 mm diameter) is placed discreetly in the lower‑left corner, partially hidden by a soft shadow, to give a sense of size without pulling focus. |

### 2. Materials & Textures  

* **Body polymer** – matte‑black, low‑gloss, brushed‑metal micro‑texture (≈10 µm roughness). No visible fingerprints.  
* **Honeycomb cells** – deeper than the surrounding surface, creating micro‑shadows; interior walls are the same matte black but appear slightly darker due to depth.  
* **RGB strip** – silicone‑encapsulated LEDs, diffused to avoid hot spots; the cyan glow is uniform.  
* **USB‑C port housing** – brushed aluminum (light‑gray), with a faint specular highlight (≈10% reflectivity).  
* **Buttons** – dual‑finish: matte body with a semi‑gloss edge (≈20% reflectivity) to define the button shape.  
* **DPI LED ring** – thin cyan line (≈0.5 mm thick) that appears as a soft halo.  
* **Paracord cable** – woven nylon, dark‑gray, with a subtle sheen where fibers cross; the braid pattern is visible at 2× magnification.  
* **Surface** – a matte‑black “invisible” acrylic platform with a thin, non‑reflective anti‑glare coating, allowing the mouse to appear to float.

### 3. Lighting Setup  

| Light | Position | Type | Settings | Effect |
|-------|----------|------|----------|--------|
| **Key Light** | 45° left‑front, 30° above the mouse, 1.5 m away | Large softbox (60 cm × 60 cm) with diffusion panel | 5600 K, 1/200 s, f/2.8, ISO 100 | Provides the main illumination, gently wrapping around the left side, highlighting the cyan strip and revealing the honeycomb depth. |
| **Rim Light (Backlight)** | Directly behind the mouse, 10° upward, 2 m away | Grid spot (30 cm) with a barn doors | 5600 K, 1/250 s, f/4, ISO 100 | Creates a thin, cyan‑tinted halo around the left edge, emphasizing the side profile and the RGB strip. |
| **Fill Light** | 60° right‑front, 20° above, 2 m away | Small octabox (30 cm) with a white diffuser | 5600 K, 1/250 s, f/4, ISO 100, reduced to 1/4 power | Softens shadows on the right side, ensuring the matte black does not become too flat. |
| **Accent Light** | Directly above, 90° down, 1 m away | Narrow “hair” light with a snoot | 5600 K, 1/500 s, f/5.6, ISO 100 | Highlights the USB‑C port and the DPI LED ring, creating a tiny specular sparkle on the metal housing. |
| **Background Light** | 2 m behind, aimed at the backdrop | Soft, large LED panel with a very low‑intensity warm wash (3000 K) | 1/500 s, f/8, ISO 100 | Gives the matte‑black background a faint, almost imperceptible gradient from dark charcoal at the bottom to deep midnight blue at the top, adding depth without competing with the cyan. |

All lights are diffused to avoid harsh specular highlights; the only crisp reflections are the brushed‑aluminum port and the tiny LED ring.

### 4. Camera & Lens  

* **Camera body** – Full‑frame DSLR or mirrorless (e.g., Sony α7R IV, Canon R5).  
* **Lens** – 100 mm macro lens, f/2.8, 1:1 reproduction ratio.  
* **Aperture** – f/2.8 to give a shallow depth of field that keeps the front‑most side buttons and the left side strip razor‑sharp while gently blurring the far‑right edge and the background.  
* **Shutter speed** – 1/200 s (synchronised with the flash units).  
* **ISO** – 100 for maximum detail and minimal noise.  
* **Focus point** – Single‑point AF on the left side strip, ensuring the cyan glow is perfectly in focus.  
* **White balance** – Custom Kelvin set to 5600 K (daylight) to keep the cyan true and the matte black neutral.  
* **Image format** – 48‑MP RAW, later processed to a high‑resolution 8K PNG for maximum detail.

---

### 5. Background & Environment  

* **Backdrop** – Seamless matte‑black vinyl with a subtle, non‑reflective texture (almost imperceptible micro‑grain). The backdrop extends beyond the frame to avoid any visible edges.  
* **Floor** – Same material as the backdrop, creating a “floating” illusion.  
* **Gradient** – A very gentle vertical gradient (dark charcoal → deep midnight blue) is added in post‑production to give the scene a sense of depth while keeping the focus on the mouse.  
* **Vignette** – Slight, natural vignette from the lens, darkening the corners just enough to draw the eye to the center.  
* **No props** – Apart from the quarter for scale, the scene is clean; no logos, no text, no additional objects.

---

### 6. Post‑Processing Details  

1. **Sharpening** – Apply selective high‑frequency sharpening to the honeycomb cells, side strip, and button edges.  
2. **Noise reduction** – Minimal, only to smooth the matte‑black areas without losing texture.  
3. **Color grading** – Keep cyan at 100 % saturation, boost the black channel slightly to deepen the matte finish.  
4. **HDR blend** – Combine three exposures (‑1 EV, 0 EV, +1 EV) to retain detail in the shadows of the honeycomb while preserving the bright cyan strip.  
5. **Lens correction** – Remove any barrel distortion from the macro lens.  
6. **Reflection removal** – Use a clone stamp to eliminate any stray reflections on the brushed‑aluminum port that are not part of the intentional specular highlight.  
7. **Final output** – 8000 × 6000 px, 300 dpi, color‑managed sRGB/AdobeRGB for web and print.
