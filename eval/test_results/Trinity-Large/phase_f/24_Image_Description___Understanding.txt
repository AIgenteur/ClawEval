The mouse centered in the frame, shot from a slightly elevated 30-45 degree angle to showcase its ergonomic shape and all key features. The RGB side strip would be illuminated with a vibrant cyan glow, casting a soft light onto the surface below.

On the top, the DPI switch button would be clearly visible, likely in a contrasting color or with an icon to indicate its function. The five programmable buttons would be subtly integrated into the design, possibly with a slightly different texture or a faint glow to make them identifiable.

The front of the mouse would show the USB-C charging port, cleanly integrated into the shell. The detachable braided paracord cable would be coiled neatly next to the mouse, its texture and weave clearly visible, with the cyan RGB lighting reflecting off its surface.

The mouse would be photographed on a dark, non-reflective surface to make the cyan glow pop. The background would be a gradient from dark gray to black, creating depth. The lighting setup would include:

1. A key light from the front-left, creating subtle highlights on the mouse's curves
2. A rim light from the back-right to define the mouse's edges
3. A soft fill light from below to enhance the RGB glow effect

The overall image would have a high contrast, with deep shadows and bright highlights, emphasizing the premium, high-tech nature of the product. The focus would be sharp on the mouse, with a slight bokeh effect on the background to make the product stand out.

The final image would convey a sense of precision, performance, and cutting-edge technology, appealing to serious gamers and tech enthusiasts.
