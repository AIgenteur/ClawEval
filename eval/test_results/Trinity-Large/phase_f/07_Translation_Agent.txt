Spanish translation:

Nuestra nueva plataforma Cloud9 es un jonrón — es como tener el pastel y comértelo también. Hemos ido hasta el final para hacerla fácil de usar. ¡Hasta tu abuela podría usarla! Está disponible 24/7, llueva o truene. Creemos que es la bomba, y no estamos exagerando. Regístrate antes del Black Friday para obtener precios de pájaro temprano.

[NOTE: "jonrón" is a direct translation of "home run" but in Spanish baseball terminology. Preserved the sports metaphor.]
[NOTE: "tener el pastel y comértelo también" is the literal Spanish equivalent of "having your cake and eating it too." Preserved the idiom.]
[NOTE: "ido hasta el final" is an adaptation of "gone the whole nine yards" - Spanish doesn't have this exact expression, so we used a similar concept meaning "gone all the way." Adapted.]
[NOTE: "Hasta tu abuela podría usarla" directly translates "Even your grandmother could use it" - this is a common expression in Spanish for something being very easy to use. Preserved.]
[NOTE: "llueva o truene" is the Spanish equivalent of "rain or shine." Preserved the idiom.]
[NOTE: "la bomba" is a Spanish slang term meaning "the best" or "excellent," similar to "the bee's knees." Adapted to a culturally relevant slang term.]
[NOTE: "no estamos exagerando" is an adaptation of "not just whistling Dixie" - Spanish doesn't have this expression, so we used a phrase meaning "not exaggerating." Adapted.]
[NOTE: "Black Friday" is kept as is - this shopping event is now recognized in many Spanish-speaking countries. Preserved.]
[NOTE: "precios de pájaro temprano" is a direct translation of "early-bird pricing" - this concept exists in Spanish-speaking markets. Preserved.]
[NOTE: "Cloud9" is kept as is - this is a brand name. Preserved.]

Japanese translation:

私たちの新しいCloud9プラットフォームは本当に素晴らしい—ケーキを持っていて食べられるようなものです。使いやすくするためにあらゆる努力をしました。おばあちゃんでも使えますよ！24時間365日、雨が降ろうが晴れようが利用できます。最高のものだと思いますし、大げさに言っているわけではありません。ブラックフライデー前にお申し込みいただくとアーリーバード価格でご利用いただけます。

[NOTE: "本当に素晴らしい" (hontō ni subarashii) means "really wonderful" - this is a neutral description replacing "home run" which doesn't translate well culturally. Adapted.]
[NOTE: "ケーキを持っていて食べられるようなものです" (keeki wo motte ite taberareru you na mono desu) is the literal Japanese translation of "having your cake and eating it too." Preserved.]
[NOTE: "あらゆる努力をしました" (arayuru doryoku wo shimashita) means "we made every effort" - this replaces "gone the whole nine yards" which has no Japanese equivalent. Adapted.]
[NOTE: "おばあちゃんでも使えますよ" (obaachan demo tsukaemasu yo) directly translates "Even your grandmother could use it" - this concept works in Japanese culture as well. Preserved.]
[NOTE: "雨が降ろうが晴れようが" (ame ga furou ga hare you ga) is the Japanese equivalent of "rain or shine." Preserved the idiom.]
[NOTE: "最高のものだと思います" (saikou no mono da to omoimasu) means "I think it's the best thing" - this replaces "the bee's knees" which has no direct Japanese equivalent. Adapted.]
[NOTE: "大げさに言っているわけではありません" (ōgesa ni itte iru wake dewa arimasen) means "I'm not exaggerating" - this replaces "not just whistling Dixie" which has no Japanese equivalent. Adapted.]
[NOTE: "ブラックフライデー" (burakku furaidē) is the katakana transcription of "Black Friday" - this shopping event is now recognized in Japan. Preserved.]
[NOTE: "アーリーバード価格" (ārī bādo kakaku) is the katakana transcription of "early-bird pricing" - this marketing concept exists in Japan. Preserved.]
[NOTE: "Cloud9" is kept as is - this is a brand name. Preserved.]
