To generate a high-end, professional image prompt, you need to move beyond simple descriptions and use the language of a commercial photographer. Here is the precise visual breakdown for a "premium wireless gaming mouse" shot.

### 1. Visual Layout & Composition
*   **Perspective:** A "Hero Shot" taken from a low-profile, 3/4 isometric angle. The mouse is positioned slightly off-center to the right, angled so the viewer sees the top surface, the front (showing the USB-C port), and the left side (showcasing the RGB strip).
*   **Framing:** A tight, macro-focused composition. The mouse occupies about 60% of the frame. The detachable braided paracord cable is elegantly coiled in a soft "S" curve in the foreground/side, leading the eye toward the mouse, but slightly out of focus to create depth.
*   **Depth of Field:** Extremely shallow (f/2.8 equivalent). The primary focus is sharp on the texture of the honeycomb shell and the DPI button, while the rear of the mouse and the coiled cable melt into a smooth, creamy bokeh.

### 2. Materials & Textures
*   **The Shell:** An obsidian-black, ultra-matte "soft-touch" finish that absorbs light rather than reflecting it. The surface should look velvety and premium, not plastic-like.
*   **The Honeycomb:** An intricate, geometric hexagonal perforation pattern across the top and sides. The edges of the hexagons should be crisp, with deep shadows inside the holes to emphasize the "ultra-light" hollow construction.
*   **The Buttons:** The main left/right clicks should have a very subtle, micro-textured grain to suggest tactile grip. The DPI switch on top should be a slightly different material—perhaps a smooth, satin-finish black to distinguish it.
*   **The Cable:** A high-detail macro view of the braided paracord. You should see the individual micro-weave of the fabric, giving it a rugged, high-tech textile appearance.
*   **The RGB:** A vibrant, electric cyan light strip embedded in the side. The light should not be a harsh line; it should have a "soft bloom" effect, casting a faint cyan glow onto the matte black surface immediately adjacent to the strip.

### 3. Lighting Setup
*   **Key Light:** A large, softbox positioned from the top-left, providing a gentle gradient across the matte surface to define the mouse's ergonomic curves without creating harsh specular highlights.
*   **Rim Lighting (The "Edge" Light):** A sharp, cool-white strip light positioned behind the mouse to create a thin, brilliant highlight along the silhouette. This separates the black mouse from the dark background and defines the 126mm length.
*   **Accent Lighting:** The cyan RGB strip acts as a secondary light source, providing a color contrast (Cyan vs. Neutral White/Black) that adds "pop" and a sense of energy.
*   **Shadows:** Soft, diffused contact shadows where the mouse meets the surface, giving it a sense of weight and presence despite its 67g specification.

### 4. Camera Angle & Technical Specs
*   **Angle:** Low-angle macro shot (approx. 15 degrees above the surface). This makes the mouse look "monumental" and powerful.
*   **Lens Simulation:** 85mm or 100mm Macro lens. This eliminates distortion and ensures the proportions (126 x 64 x 40mm) look realistic and professional.
*   **Color Grade:** High contrast, "Tech-Noir" aesthetic. Deep blacks, desaturated surroundings, and highly saturated cyan accents.

### 5. Background & Environment
*   **Surface:** A dark, minimalist, non-reflective slate or brushed carbon fiber desk mat. It should be dark enough to let the mouse be the star, but textured enough to catch a hint of the cyan RGB glow.
*   **Atmosphere:** Clean, sterile, and premium. No dust, no fingerprints, no clutter. A sense of "high-end gaming studio" or "minimalist workstation."
