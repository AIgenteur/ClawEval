### Summary of Adaptation Strategies

| Idiom / Reference | Strategy Used | Reasoning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Home run** | **Adapted/Replaced** | While baseball is known in both regions, the business metaphor is stronger as "great success" (Éxito rotundo / 大ヒット) rather than a literal sports term. |
| **Cake / Eating it** | **Replaced** | The specific imagery of the cake doesn't translate as a fixed idiom. Replaced with functional equivalents meaning "having it all" (Todo sin renunciar / 良いとこ取り). |
| **Whole nine yards** | **Replaced** | Obscure origin even in English. Replaced with standard phrases for "doing everything possible" (Todo lo posible / 全力を尽くしました). |
| **Grandmother** | **Preserved** | Universal archetype for non-tech-savvy users across cultures. |
| **Rain or shine** | **Adapted** | Direct equivalent idioms exist in both languages (Llueva o truene / 雨の日も晴れの日も) that convey the exact same sentiment naturally. |
| **Bee's knees** | **Replaced** | 1920s US slang with no logical meaning in other languages. Replaced with generic superlatives (Lo máximo / 最高). |
| **Whistling Dixie** | **Replaced** | Highly specific US Southern historical reference. Completely unintelligible elsewhere. Replaced with assertions of honesty (No hablamos por hablar / 嘘はついていません). |
| **Cloud9** | **Preserved** | Treated as a proper noun/brand name, though the underlying concept of "heavenly bliss" is understood globally. |
| **Black Friday** | **Preserved/Localized** | The event is globally recognized. Translated directly or transliterated (Viernes Negro / ブラックフライデー) as it is a established commercial term. |
| **Early-bird** | **Adapted** | The bird metaphor is not standard for sales discounts in these cultures. Adapted to "first customers" or "early reservation" (Primeros clientes / 早期割引). |
