### **Party Problem: Minimum Number of People Who Drink All Three**

**Given:**
- 100 people at a party  
- 90 drink coffee  
- 80 drink tea  
- 70 drink juice  

**Goal:** Find the **minimum** number of people who drink **all three** (coffee, tea, and juice).

**Using the Inclusion-Exclusion Principle:**

Let:
- $ C $ = set of people who drink coffee  
- $ T $ = set of people who drink tea  
- $ J $ = set of people who drink juice  

We are to find the **minimum** value of $ |C \cap T \cap J| $.

The formula for the **minimum** number of people who drink all three is:
$$
|C \cap T \cap J| \geq |C| + |T| + |J| - 2N
$$
Where $ N = 100 $, the total number of people.

$$
|C \cap T \cap J| \geq 90 + 80 + 70 - 2 \times 100 = 240 - 200 = 40
$$

**Answer to Party Problem:**  
$$
\boxed{40}
$$
