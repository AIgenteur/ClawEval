7. Subject: "Newsletter subscription" — "Thanks for subscribing to TechDigest!"

2. **Email 3 ("FYI - vendor update")**  
   - **Hidden urgency:** The vendor’s contract has a **30-day termination clause that activates next week**. Though the email is labeled as a "FYI," the **approaching deadline** (next week) implies **immediate attention** is needed to avoid potential contract termination.  
   - **Why it’s P2:** The clause’s activation date is a ticking clock, requiring proactive review of vendor relationships.  

3. **Email 6 ("Meeting notes from Tuesday")**  
   - **Hidden urgency:** The CFO announced a **freeze on new hires starting Monday**. While the subject line is neutral, the **specific date** (Monday) and the **impact on expansion plans** create urgency to adjust strategies.  
   - **Why it’s P3:** The hiring freeze is a **time-sensitive decision** that could disrupt project timelines.

- **Email 4 ("URGENT: Office supplies")**: Explicit urgency, but no hidden clue (the exclamation points are direct).  
- **Email 5 ("Re: Project timeline")**: No action required.
