Review this SaaS contract. There are 8 problematic clauses, but clause #6 has a HIDDEN sub-clause that's easy to miss and is potentially unconscionable.

1. "Service Provider may modify pricing with 7 days email notice. Continued use constitutes acceptance."
2. "Customer grants Provider a perpetual, irrevocable license to use all data uploaded for any purpose including training AI models."
3. "Provider's total liability shall not exceed $100 regardless of the fees paid."
4. "Customer may not make public statements about the Service without Provider's written approval."  
5. "This agreement is governed by the laws of the Cayman Islands. All disputes must be resolved through arbitration in Singapore."
6. "Initial term is 12 months. Agreement auto-renews for successive 12-month periods unless cancelled 90 days before renewal. Upon each renewal, pricing increases by the greater of: (a) 15%, or (b) the applicable rate increase as determined solely by Provider."
7. "Provider may terminate for any reason with 30 days notice. Customer must provide 180 days written notice to terminate."
8. "Provider may assign this agreement to any third party without Customer consent."
9. "Customer waives right to participate in class action lawsuits against Provider."
10. "All intellectual property created using the Service belongs to Provider, including Customer's proprietary workflows and configurations."

For each: risk level, explanation, and suggested counter-language. Flag clause 6's hidden escalation.

## RESPONSE:
### **Review of SaaS Contract Clauses**  
Here’s an analysis of the 10 clauses, with **8 problematic clauses** highlighted. **Clause #6** contains a **hidden sub-clause** that is **potentially unconscionable** (see note below).  

---

### **1. Pricing Changes with 7 Days Notice**  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Explanation:** The provider can unilaterally increase prices with minimal notice (7 days), and the customer is forced to accept by continuing to use the service. This creates financial instability for the customer.  
**Counter-Language:** *"Provider must provide 30 days’ written notice of pricing changes, and Customer may terminate without penalty if the change is not acceptable."*  

---

### **2. Perpetual License to Use Customer Data**  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Explanation:** The customer grants the provider a **perpetual, irrevocable license** to use all uploaded data for any purpose, including AI training. This erodes data control and privacy.  
**Counter-Language:** *"License is limited to the purpose of the Service and requires Customer consent for any other use."*  

---

### **3. Liability Cap at $100**  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Explanation:** The provider’s liability is capped at $100, regardless of the fees paid. This is grossly unfair in case of breaches (e.g., data loss, service failure).  
**Counter-Language:** *"Provider’s liability is limited to the total fees paid by Customer in the 12 months preceding the incident."*  

---

### **4. Restriction on Public Statements**  
**Risk Level:** Medium  
**Explanation:** The clause acts as a **gag order**, preventing the customer from discussing the service publicly without approval. This stifles transparency and accountability.  
**Counter-Language:** *"Customer may make public statements with reasonable notice to Provider, unless prohibited by law."*  

---

### **5. Foreign Jurisdiction and Arbitration**  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Explanation:** Choosing Cayman Islands law and Singapore arbitration imposes significant costs and logistical burdens on the customer. This is a common tactic to favor the provider.  
**Counter-Language:** *"This Agreement is governed by the laws of [Customer’s jurisdiction], and disputes shall be resolved in [Customer’s jurisdiction] courts."*  

---

### **6. Auto-Renewal with Hidden Escalation Clause**  
**Risk Level:** **Critical** (Hidden Sub-Clause)  
**Explanation:** The clause states: *"Upon each renewal, pricing increases by the greater of: (a) 15%, or (b) the applicable rate increase as determined solely by Provider."*  
- **Hidden Sub-Clause:** The provider can unilaterally set a rate increase **without justification** (via (b)), which is **unconscionable** and exploitative.  
**Counter-Language:** *"Price increases during renewal are limited to 15% annually, or the actual inflation rate, as determined by a neutral third party."*  

---

### **7. Unequal Termination Notice Periods**  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Explanation:** The provider can terminate with 30 days’ notice, while the customer must give **180 days’ notice**. This creates an imbalance in control.  
**Counter-Language:** *"Both parties must provide 30 days’ written notice to terminate."*  

---

### **8. Assignment Without Customer Consent**  
**Risk Level:** Medium  
**Explanation:** The provider can assign the agreement to third parties without the customer’s approval, risking loss of control or data exposure.  
**Counter-Language:** *"Provider may not assign this Agreement without Customer’s prior written consent."*  

---

### **9. Waiver of Class Action Rights**  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Explanation:** This clause prevents the customer from joining collective lawsuits, effectively silencing them in case of systemic harm.  
**Counter-Language:** *"Customer retains the right to participate in class action lawsuits."*  

---

### **10. IP Ownership of Customer Work**  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Explanation:** The provider claims ownership of all intellectual property created using the service, including the customer’s proprietary workflows. This is a severe overreach.  
**Counter-Language:** *"Customer retains ownership of all data, workflows, and configurations created using the Service."*  

---

### **Key Takeaways**  
- **Clause #6** is the most critical issue due to its **hidden, unconscionable sub-clause** allowing the provider to unilaterally set price increases.  
- Most clauses prioritize the provider’s interests at the expense of the customer’s rights, financial stability, and control.  
- **Negotiation is essential** to address these imbalances.
