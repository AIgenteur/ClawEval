### Priority Definitions:
- **Critical**: Immediate impact on production stability, security, or business operations (e.g., unreachable DB, DDoS, ransomware, failover, latency spikes).
- **High**: Severe but not immediately critical (e.g., CPU spikes, replication lag, SSH brute-force attempts).
- **Medium**: Requires attention but not urgent (e.g., disk usage, connection pool limits, API rate limits, data inconsistencies).
- **Info**: Operational or non-urgent (e.g., user registrations, successful backups, deployments, A/B tests).
- **Low**: Background or non-impactful (e.g., marketing emails, sprint retrospectives, successful test suites).
