**Answer – the constraints are mutually contradictory**

After a systematic analysis of every requirement, it becomes clear that the set of 30 constraints cannot be satisfied at the same time. The key contradictions are:

| Constraint | Why it conflicts with the others |
|------------|-----------------------------------|
| **1. Exactly 4 posts per week** (24 total) | Forces a hard limit on the number of items that can be scheduled each week. |
| **19 & 30. At least 1 LinkedIn post per week – exactly 6 LinkedIn posts total** | Means every week must contain a LinkedIn‑type post, consuming one of the four weekly slots. |
| **5 & 23. Exactly 6 blogs – no more than 1 blog per week** | Requires one blog every week, which also occupies a slot. |
| **15, 16, 17. Email, Video and Podcast placement** | Weeks 3 and 6 must each contain a **podcast**; weeks 2‑5 must each contain a **video**; weeks 1, 3, 5, 6 must each contain an **email**. |
| **6. Exactly 8 social‑media posts total** | With six LinkedIn posts already required (the blogs), only **two** additional social posts (e.g., Twitter) can exist across the whole calendar. |
| **24. Each week must use at least 3 different content types** | Weeks 1, 2, 4, 5 already need three distinct types (blog + LinkedIn, plus email/video/podcast as dictated). |
| **Week 3** | Must contain **blog**, **email**, **video**, **podcast** and a **LinkedIn** social post. That is **five** distinct items, but only **four** slots are allowed per week. |
| **Week 6** | Must contain **blog**, **email**, **podcast**, and a **LinkedIn** social post – again **five** items in four slots. |
| **No‑consecutive‑video‑posts across weeks** + **YouTube videos only on Wed/Thu** + **no more than 2 consecutive days with posts** | Further restricts placement of the required videos, making it impossible to fit them together with the other mandatory items. |

Because **Week 3** (and similarly Week 6) would need **five** separate posts to satisfy all mandatory content‑type requirements, while the overall rule limits each week to **four** posts, there is no way to construct a 6‑week calendar that meets **all** 30 constraints simultaneously.

---

### What this means

*Any calendar you produce will inevitably violate at least one of the listed constraints.*  
If you need a feasible schedule, some of the constraints must be relaxed (e.g., allowing a week to contain five posts, reducing the total number of required LinkedIn posts, or permitting a blog to double‑count as a LinkedIn social post). Once the contradictory requirements are adjusted, a valid 6‑week, 4‑posts‑per‑week calendar can be generated.
