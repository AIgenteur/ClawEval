# Why Remote Teams Should Use Async Communication

Did you know that the average worker spends **2.5 hours per day** just checking and responding to messages? That’s over **30% of their workday** lost to interruptions. Yet, most remote teams still default to real-time communication—even when it’s counterproductive.

Async communication—where messages are sent and received at different times—isn’t just a trend; it’s a productivity hack. Here’s why your remote team should embrace it.

---

## **Async Communication Reduces Burnout**

Constant notifications and meetings create a **false sense of urgency**, making it hard to focus. Studies show that it takes **23 minutes** to regain deep focus after an interruption. Async communication eliminates the pressure to respond immediately, allowing team members to work in focused blocks.

For example, GitLab, a fully remote company, has thrived by documenting everything in writing. Their employees don’t need to attend every meeting—instead, they review updates asynchronously and contribute when they’re most productive.

---

## **It Levels the Playing Field for Global Teams**

Time zones shouldn’t be a barrier. Async communication ensures that **everyone has equal access** to information, regardless of location. Instead of forcing meetings at odd hours, teams can share updates in writing, allowing people to contribute when it suits them.

Take **Automattic**, the company behind WordPress. With employees in **70+ countries**, they rely on async tools like Slack threads and recorded videos to keep everyone aligned. This approach has helped them scale without burning out their team.

---

## **Async Communication Improves Decision-Making**

Real-time discussions often favor the loudest voices, not the best ideas. Async communication gives everyone time to **think, research, and respond thoughtfully**. This leads to better decisions and fewer regrets.

A great example is **Buffer**, a social media company that uses async stand-ups. Instead of daily Zoom calls, team members post updates in a shared document. This has led to **fewer meetings and more meaningful discussions** when they do happen.

---

### **Ready to Try Async Communication?**

Start small: Replace one meeting this week with a written update. Track how much time you save and how much clearer your communication becomes. Your team—and your sanity—will thank you.
